# **Cuban Dominoes Lingo and Feature Integrations**

### **General Terms**

1. **La Salida**:  
   * The opening tile of the game or round.  
   * Often accompanied by strategic emphasis: "¡No me comas la salida\!" (Don’t ruin my opening\!)  
2. **Capicúa**:  
   * A play that closes a round by fitting the last tile on both ends of the chain.  
   * A celebrated move, often met with cheers or playful taunts.  
3. **Pollona**:  
   * Winning a game where the opponent scores zero points.  
   * A moment for bragging rights: "¡Te di una pollona\!" (I gave you a shutout\!)  
4. **Viajeras**:  
   * Scoring 100+ points in a single round.  
   * A sign of dominance and strategy.  
5. **Poner una tranca**:  
   * To block the game and prevent further play.  
   * Equivalent to creating a “locked” game state.

---

### **Player-Specific Slang**

6. **El Viejo**:  
   * Refers to an older, experienced player who is considered wise or tricky.  
   * Respectfully: "Cuidado con el viejo, que sabe más que tú." (Watch out for the old guy, he knows more than you.)  
7. **El Comelón**:  
   * A player who “eats” (plays over) their partner’s opening tile or strategy.  
   * Critically: "¡Eres un comelón\!" (You’re ruining the strategy\!)  
8. **El Pesado**:  
   * A player who holds on to high-pip tiles for too long, risking penalties in blocked games.  
   * Teased: "Deja de ser tan pesado." (Stop holding back.)  
9. **El Traidor**:  
   * A player accused of sabotaging their team, often unintentionally.  
   * Lightheartedly: "¡Eres un traidor\!" (You’re a traitor\!)

---

### **Tiles and Numbers**

10. **Las Blancas**:  
    * Refers to tiles with no pips (the blank side).  
    * “No tires blancas si no tienes plan.” (Don’t throw blanks without a plan.)  
11. **Los Picos**:  
    * High-pip tiles like \[6|6\], \[9|9\], etc.  
    * Mentioned when discussing strategy or penalties: "Ese pico te costó el juego." (That high tile cost you the game.)  
12. **La Mula**:  
    * Doubles that are hard to play, often causing blocks.  
    * “Esa mula no camina.” (That mule doesn’t move.)  
13. **La Carreta**:  
    * Refers to “rolling” a strategy forward by extending plays.  
    * Encouraging: "¡Sigue con la carreta\!" (Keep it rolling\!)

---

### **Play Actions**

14. **Darle Agua**:  
    * Shuffling the tiles at the start of the game.  
    * Commonly said: "Vamos, dale agua bien." (Come on, shuffle well.)  
15. **Tirar un veneno**:  
    * Playing a tile to “poison” the game for your opponents, forcing bad moves or passes.  
    * “Le tiré un veneno y no pudo jugar.” (I played poison, and they couldn’t move.)  
16. **Trancar el dominó**:  
    * Blocking the domino chain, usually with a strategic double or high pip.  
    * Teased: "¡Trancaste y perdiste puntos\!" (You blocked and lost points\!)  
17. **Pegar el dominó**:  
    * Winning a round by playing your last tile.  
    * Celebratory: "¡Pegó el dominó\!" (They won the round\!)

---

### **Gameplay Commentary**

18. **Hablar Mierda**:  
    * Trash-talking during the game.  
    * Integral to Cuban Domino culture, but meant to be lighthearted.  
19. **Me comieron vivo**:  
    * “They ate me alive\!” Refers to being dominated in a game.  
    * Used jokingly after a bad loss.  
20. **¡Coge eso\!**:  
    * "Take that\!" A playful taunt after making a good move or forcing a pass.  
21. **Se quedó pegado**:  
    * "They got stuck." Refers to a player unable to make a move.  
22. **Me pasaron la cuenta**:  
    * "They made me pay for it." Used when a risky move backfires.

---

### **Team Play**

23. **Déjame el camino abierto**:  
    * "Leave the path open for me." Asking your partner to make strategic plays for teamwork.  
24. **Comerse la jugada**:  
    * When a teammate ruins a winning strategy by playing the wrong tile.  
    * Critically: "Te comiste la jugada." (You messed up the play.)  
25. **Amarrar al otro equipo**:  
    * To strategically trap or block the other team’s options.  
    * "Los amarramos bien." (We trapped them well.)

---

### **Fun and Celebration**

26. **El Que No Llora No Mama**:  
    * “The squeaky wheel gets the grease.” Refers to pleading or joking to influence play.  
27. **¡Jaque Mate\!**:  
    * "Checkmate\!" Used jokingly to mimic chess, after a decisive win.  
28. **Cantar Victoria**:  
    * To prematurely celebrate, often leading to embarrassment if the game flips.  
    * "No cantes victoria todavía." (Don’t celebrate too soon.)
